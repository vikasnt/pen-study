# PEN Study — Cryogenic Detector Coating Stability

Large-scale characterisation of PEN (polyethylene naphthalate) as a 
wavelength-shifting material in a 2-tonne liquid argon detector at CERN.

Light yield measured daily over 12 days of continuous cryogenic operation, 
corresponding to 1-3 years of equivalent deep-underground detector runtime.

**Key result:** Demonstrated stable performance of PEN as a detector 
coating under extended cryogenic conditions, supporting its adoption in 
next-generation liquid argon detectors.
